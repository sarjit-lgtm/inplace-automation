## Main Content

### What Custom Cabinetry Means at Inplace Studio

There is a meaningful difference between cabinets that fit a space and cabinetry that was conceived for it. At Inplace Studio, every cabinet we deliver is made to order. We hold no inventory. Nothing is pulled from a warehouse or adjusted from a standard size. Each piece is designed around your kitchen's exact dimensions, your aesthetic preferences, and the way you actually use your home.

This is what separates true custom cabinetry from the stock and semi-custom options available through big-box retailers and general contractors. Stock cabinetry is manufactured in fixed sizes and limited finishes — it is built for speed and volume, not for the nuances of a specific home. Semi-custom offers some flexibility in sizing and finish selection, but still operates within the constraints of a predetermined catalog. Custom cabinetry starts with a blank page. Every dimension, every material, every detail is determined by your project — not by what happens to be available.

### The Made-to-Order Process

Our process begins with a one-on-one design consultation at our Southern California showroom. Your dedicated designer works with you to understand the full scope of your vision — from the overall style of the kitchen to the interior configuration of every drawer and cabinet. Precise measurements of your space are taken, and detailed 3D renderings are produced so you can see exactly how the finished cabinetry will look and function before a single order is placed.

Once the design is finalized, we select the ideal manufacturer for your project and place the order. Every component is built specifically for your kitchen. Depending on the supplier and scope, production typically takes eight to sixteen weeks. When the cabinetry arrives, our expert installation team ensures a flawless fit — no shimming, no filler strips, no compromises.

### Materials, Finishes, and Door Styles

Working with Inplace Studio gives you access to an extraordinary range of materials and finishes. Whether you are drawn to the clean minimalism of a lacquered, handleless German kitchen or the rich warmth of hand-selected hardwoods with an inset door style, we source materials that meet the highest standards of durability and beauty.

Our finish options span matte and high-gloss lacquers, natural wood veneers, custom paint-matched colors, metal accents, glass inserts, and textured laminates engineered for longevity. Door styles range from flat slab and shaker profiles to fully bespoke designs developed in collaboration with our manufacturing partners.

### World-Class Supplier Partnerships

We have cultivated relationships with some of the most respected cabinetry manufacturers in the world. Our supplier portfolio includes SieMatic, known for its precision-engineered German cabinetry; Woodmode, one of America's premier custom cabinet makers; Renzo Restelli, specialists in Italian-crafted luxury; and Signature Custom Cabinetry, which delivers exceptional quality at a refined price point. We also work with locally based craftsmen for fully bespoke commissions when a project demands something entirely unique.

These partnerships are not decorative. They give our clients access to proprietary finishes, hardware systems, and construction methods that are simply unavailable through standard channels.

### Why Custom Cabinetry Is Worth the Investment

A kitchen built with custom cabinetry is not just more beautiful — it is more functional, more durable, and ultimately more valuable. Precision-fit cabinets eliminate the wasted space that comes with standard sizes. Superior construction methods and premium materials ensure the cabinetry will perform and look exceptional for decades. And because the design is wholly yours, the result is a kitchen that feels intentional in every detail.

For homeowners investing in a kitchen that reflects the quality of the rest of their home, custom cabinetry is not a luxury — it is the standard.

### Key Features

- **Precision Fit:** Every cabinet built to your kitchen's exact measurements — no fillers, no gaps, no wasted space
- **Unlimited Finish Options:** Lacquers, veneers, custom paint matches, metals, glass, and specialty textures
- **Premium Hardware:** Soft-close hinges, full-extension drawer slides, and integrated organizational systems from leading European manufacturers
- **Lifetime Quality:** Solid wood construction, dovetail joinery, and finishes engineered to resist wear, moisture, and UV exposure
- **Interior Customization:** Drawer dividers, pull-out shelving, appliance garages, spice racks, and bespoke storage solutions designed around your routine
- **Access to World-Class Brands:** SieMatic, Woodmode, Renzo Restelli, Signature Custom Cabinetry, and select local artisans

## FAQ Section

### How long does custom cabinetry take?

The timeline for custom cabinetry depends on the complexity of the design and the manufacturer selected for your project. In general, the design phase takes two to four weeks, followed by a production period of eight to sixteen weeks. Installation typically requires one to two weeks. From your initial consultation to completed installation, most projects are finished within four to six months. Your project manager will provide a detailed timeline at the outset so you know exactly what to expect at every stage.

### What is the difference between custom and semi-custom cabinetry?

Semi-custom cabinetry allows you to choose from a set menu of sizes, finishes, and configurations offered by a manufacturer. It provides more flexibility than stock cabinetry but still operates within predefined parameters. Custom cabinetry has no such constraints — every dimension, material, finish, and interior configuration is designed specifically for your project. This means your cabinets fit your space perfectly, reflect your personal style precisely, and include storage solutions tailored to the way you live. The difference is evident in both the finished appearance and the day-to-day functionality of the kitchen.

### Which suppliers do you work with?

Inplace Studio partners with a curated selection of world-class manufacturers, including SieMatic, Woodmode, Renzo Restelli, and Signature Custom Cabinetry. We also collaborate with locally based craftsmen for fully bespoke commissions. During the design process, your designer will recommend the supplier best suited to your project's aesthetic goals, functional requirements, and budget. Each of our partners has been selected for their quality of construction, breadth of material options, and reliability of delivery.

### Can I see material samples before making a decision?

Absolutely. Our Southern California showroom is designed specifically for this purpose. You can touch and compare wood species, finish samples, hardware options, and door style profiles in person. We also maintain an extensive library of material samples from each of our supplier partners that can be reviewed during your design consultation. We encourage every client to see and feel the materials before finalizing selections — particularly for finishes where texture, sheen, and color accuracy matter.

### What is the price range for custom cabinets?

Custom cabinetry pricing varies based on the manufacturer, materials, finish complexity, and the size of the kitchen. As a general reference, custom cabinetry projects at Inplace Studio typically begin around $40,000 and can exceed $150,000 for large-scale kitchens with premium materials and complex configurations. During your initial consultation, we will discuss your goals and budget openly, and your designer will present options that align with both. We believe in complete transparency — you will receive detailed specifications and pricing before any order is placed.

### Do you offer warranties on custom cabinetry?

Yes. All cabinetry ordered through Inplace Studio carries the manufacturer's warranty, which varies by supplier but typically covers structural components and finish integrity for a period of five years to a limited lifetime. In addition, our installation workmanship is guaranteed. If any issue arises related to how the cabinetry was installed, we will address it promptly and at no additional cost. Your project manager will review the specific warranty terms applicable to your selected manufacturer before production begins.

### A Design Process Built Around You

At Inplace Studio, kitchen design is not a transaction — it is a collaboration. Every project begins with a conversation. We listen to how you use your kitchen, what frustrates you about your current space, what inspires you, and what matters most. **Material Selection.** With the layout confirmed, we move into material selection. Your designer collaborates closely with you at every stage, presenting options, explaining trade-offs, and refining the plan until it fully reflects your vision. This is not a one-meeting process — it is an ongoing partnership.

## FAQ Section

### What happens during a kitchen design consultation?

Your initial consultation is a thorough, unhurried conversation about your project. We discuss your design preferences, how you use your kitchen, what you like and dislike about your current space, your timeline, and your budget parameters. If the consultation takes place at our showroom, you will also have the opportunity to explore our displays and review material samples. If we meet at your home, we can assess the existing space directly. The consultation typically lasts sixty to ninety minutes and concludes with a clear outline of next steps. There is no obligation and no pressure — the goal is simply to determine whether Inplace Studio is the right fit for your project.

### How long does the design process take?

The design phase typically takes three to six weeks, depending on the complexity of the project and the pace of decision-making. A straightforward kitchen with a well-defined vision may reach final design approval in as few as two to three weeks. Larger projects involving layout reconfiguration, multiple material selections, or coordination with architects and contractors may require additional time. We never rush the design process — it is far better to invest an extra week in getting the plan right than to discover an issue during production or installation.

### Do you provide 3D renderings?

Yes. Photorealistic 3D renderings are a standard part of every Inplace Studio design project. These renderings show your kitchen from multiple angles, with accurate representations of the selected finishes, countertops, hardware, and lighting conditions. They are an essential tool for visualizing the completed space before any orders are placed, and they allow you to make informed adjustments while changes are still simple and cost-free. Most clients find that the renderings are the moment the project transitions from abstract concept to something they can genuinely feel excited about.

### Can I bring my own inspiration photos?

We strongly encourage it. Inspiration images — whether from design magazines, Pinterest, Instagram, or photographs of kitchens you have seen in person — are enormously valuable in the early stages of design. They help your designer understand your aesthetic language quickly and give the conversation a concrete visual foundation. Even images of kitchens you do not like are useful, as they help us understand your boundaries and preferences. Bring as many references as you have. We will work through them together and identify the elements that should inform your design.

### Do you work with architects and contractors?

Yes, and we do so regularly. Many of our projects involve coordination with the client's architect, general contractor, interior designer, or builder. We are experienced in integrating our kitchen design into a larger renovation or new construction project, and we communicate directly with your other professionals to ensure that all technical requirements — including structural, plumbing, electrical, and ventilation specifications — are addressed in the design. If you do not yet have a contractor, we can provide recommendations from our network of trusted professionals in San Diego, Los Angeles, and Orange County.

### What design styles do you specialize in?

Inplace Studio has deep experience across all major kitchen design styles, including modern, contemporary, transitional, and traditional. Our supplier relationships — particularly with European manufacturers like SieMatic and Italian makers like Renzo Restelli — give us particular strength in modern and contemporary design. Our partnerships with Woodmode and Signature Custom Cabinetry provide exceptional options for traditional and transitional kitchens. That said, we do not limit ourselves to any single aesthetic. Many of our most successful projects blend elements from multiple traditions to create something entirely personal.

### Full-Service Kitchen Remodeling

A kitchen remodel at the level Inplace Studio delivers is not a weekend project — it is a carefully orchestrated process that touches every surface, system, and detail in the room. We provide a genuinely full-service experience: design, cabinetry, countertops, hardware, backsplash, fixtures, and expert installation, all coordinated under a single point of management. There is no need to hire separate contractors for separate trades or chase down suppliers on your own. We bring everything together.

Our remodeling projects range from refined updates — replacing cabinetry, countertops, and hardware within an existing layout — to complete gut renovations that reconfigure the floor plan, relocate plumbing and electrical, and reimagine the kitchen from the ground up. Regardless of scope, the standard of execution is the same: meticulous planning, premium materials, and craftsmanship that will hold up for decades.

### What Is Included

Every Inplace Studio kitchen remodel includes:

- **Design and Planning:** A comprehensive design phase with 3D renderings and detailed specifications
- **Custom Cabinetry:** Made-to-order cabinetry from our portfolio of world-class suppliers
- **Countertop Fabrication and Installation:** Natural stone, engineered quartz, porcelain, or specialty surfaces
- **Hardware and Accessories:** Premium hinges, drawer systems, pulls, and interior organizational components
- **Backsplash and Wall Treatments:** Tile, stone, metal, or specialty materials selected to complement the design
- **Professional Installation:** Expert installers who specialize in luxury kitchen environments
- **Project Management:** A dedicated project manager who coordinates every trade, delivery, and milestone

### The Remodeling Timeline

Understanding what to expect at each stage removes the uncertainty that makes remodeling stressful. Here is the typical progression of an Inplace Studio kitchen remodel:

**Weeks 1-4: Design and Planning.** Your dedicated designer works with you to develop the complete kitchen plan, including cabinetry layout, material selections, and 3D renderings. Precise measurements are taken and all specifications are finalized.

**Weeks 4-6: Ordering and Procurement.** Once the design is approved, cabinetry is ordered from the selected manufacturer and countertop materials are reserved. Lead times for custom cabinetry typically range from eight to sixteen weeks depending on the supplier.

**Weeks 12-18: Pre-Construction Preparation.** As materials near delivery, we finalize the construction schedule, confirm subcontractor availability, and ensure all permits are in place. You will receive a detailed timeline outlining each phase of the physical work.

**Weeks 18-22: Demolition and Construction.** The existing kitchen is carefully removed and any necessary structural, plumbing, or electrical modifications are completed. This is the most disruptive phase, and it typically lasts two to four weeks.

**Weeks 22-26: Installation and Finishing.** Cabinetry is installed with precision, followed by countertop templating and installation, backsplash work, hardware mounting, and final adjustments. Every detail is inspected before we consider the project complete.

**Final Walkthrough.** Before we hand the kitchen back to you, we conduct a thorough walkthrough to verify that every element meets our standards and your expectations. Any punch-list items are addressed immediately.

### How We Manage Your Project

Every Inplace Studio remodel is overseen by a dedicated project manager who serves as your single point of contact from design approval through final walkthrough. Your project manager coordinates all trades — cabinetry installers, countertop fabricators, electricians, plumbers, tile setters, and painters — so that work proceeds on schedule and in the correct sequence.

This level of coordination is what separates a well-managed remodel from a chaotic one. You will not need to chase subcontractors, interpret construction schedules, or mediate between trades. Your project manager handles all of that, providing regular updates and being available to answer questions at every stage.

### Minimizing Disruption to Your Home

We understand that a kitchen remodel affects your daily life, and we take deliberate steps to minimize that impact. Work areas are sealed and protected to contain dust and debris. We establish clear work hours and communicate the schedule in advance. Material deliveries are coordinated to avoid unnecessary clutter in your home. And we work efficiently — not by cutting corners, but by planning thoroughly enough that delays and surprises are rare.

For most remodeling projects, you will not need to vacate your home. The most disruptive phase — demolition and rough construction — typically lasts two to four weeks. We recommend setting up a temporary kitchen station during this period and are happy to advise on how to make that transition as comfortable as possible.

### Working With Your Existing Layout

Not every kitchen remodel requires tearing everything out and starting over. If your current layout works well but the finishes, cabinetry, and surfaces need upgrading, we can execute a beautiful transformation within the existing footprint — often at a lower cost and shorter timeline than a full reconfiguration.

When a layout change is warranted — opening a wall, relocating an island, moving the sink or range — we have the expertise to manage the structural and mechanical work that comes with it. Your designer will assess whether your current layout is serving you well or whether a reconfiguration will deliver a meaningfully better result.

### Permits and Building Codes

Kitchen remodeling projects that involve electrical, plumbing, or structural modifications require permits from your local building department. Inplace Studio handles the entire permitting process on your behalf. We prepare the necessary documentation, submit permit applications, and coordinate required inspections throughout the project. You should never have to visit a building department or interpret a code requirement. That is our responsibility, and we manage it with the same level of care we bring to every other aspect of the project.

## FAQ Section

### How long does a kitchen remodel take?

A complete kitchen remodel typically takes five to seven months from initial consultation to final walkthrough. The design phase accounts for approximately one month, custom cabinetry production requires two to four months depending on the manufacturer, and the physical construction and installation phase generally takes four to eight weeks. Projects that work within an existing layout and do not require structural modifications may be completed more quickly. Your project manager will provide a detailed, phase-by-phase timeline at the outset of the project so you can plan accordingly.

### Do I need to move out during the remodel?

In most cases, no. While the kitchen will be out of commission during the demolition and installation phases — typically four to eight weeks — the rest of your home remains fully livable. We recommend setting up a temporary kitchen area with a microwave, small refrigerator, and access to a bathroom sink during this period. For particularly large-scale projects that involve work extending beyond the kitchen into adjacent spaces, a brief temporary relocation may be more comfortable, but this is the exception rather than the rule. We will advise you honestly based on the scope of your specific project.

### Do you handle permits?

Yes. Inplace Studio manages the complete permitting process for every remodeling project that requires it. This includes preparing plan documents, submitting applications to the appropriate local building department, and scheduling all required inspections throughout the course of construction. Permit requirements vary by municipality across San Diego, Los Angeles, and Orange County, and our team is experienced in navigating the specific processes for each jurisdiction. You will not need to engage with the permitting process directly unless you prefer to.

### Can you work with my existing plumbing and electrical layout?

Yes. Many of our projects are designed to work within the existing plumbing and electrical layout, which can reduce both cost and construction time. If your sink, dishwasher, and range are in locations that work well for the new design, there is often no need to relocate them. However, if the optimal design calls for moving a fixture or adding circuits — for instance, relocating an island sink or adding dedicated outlets for new appliances — we coordinate the necessary plumbing and electrical work through our network of licensed subcontractors. Your designer will discuss layout options early in the process so you can make informed decisions about scope and budget.

### What is the typical budget range for a kitchen remodel?

Kitchen remodeling budgets at Inplace Studio vary based on the scope of work, the cabinetry manufacturer selected, countertop materials, and the extent of any structural or mechanical modifications. As a general guideline, our projects typically range from $75,000 to $250,000 or more. A cabinetry and surface replacement within an existing layout falls toward the lower end of that range, while a complete gut renovation with premium materials and layout reconfiguration falls toward the upper end. During your initial consultation, we discuss budget openly and design to deliver the best possible result within your investment parameters.

### Do you offer financing?

Inplace Studio does not provide direct financing. However, many of our clients work with their banks or financial advisors to arrange home equity lines of credit, home improvement loans, or construction financing that can be applied to the project. We are happy to provide detailed project estimates and payment schedules that support your financing application. Our payment structure is transparent and milestone-based — you will always know what is due and when, and payments are tied to specific project milestones rather than arbitrary dates.
