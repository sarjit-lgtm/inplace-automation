## Stock vs. Semi-Custom vs. Full Custom: What Orange County Homeowners Should Know

Understanding the differences between cabinetry tiers is essential for making an informed investment, especially in a market like Orange County where home values and design expectations are high.

**Stock cabinetry** is mass-produced in predetermined sizes, styles, and finishes. It is the most affordable option and is available quickly, but it offers no flexibility in dimensions, materials, or configuration. Stock cabinets are suitable for rental properties, basic remodels, and budget-conscious projects, but they rarely meet the standards of luxury Orange County homes. Expect filler pieces, limited height options, and a narrow selection of door styles.

**Semi-custom cabinetry** offers more flexibility than stock. Manufacturers provide a range of door styles, finishes, and some dimensional modifications — typically in three-inch increments. Semi-custom is a step up in quality and personalization, and it works well for homes where the kitchen layout is relatively standard. However, it still operates within the manufacturer's predefined parameters, which can be limiting in homes with unusual ceiling heights, angled walls, or highly specific design visions.

**Full custom cabinetry** — the level at which Inplace Studio operates — is built entirely to your specifications. There are no predetermined sizes. Every cabinet box, door, drawer, and interior fitting is manufactured to the exact dimensions and configuration your design requires. Material choices are open-ended. Finish options are virtually unlimited. If you can envision it, a skilled custom cabinetry manufacturer can build it. For Orange County homeowners investing in a kitchen that will define their home for decades, full custom is the standard that delivers lasting satisfaction.

The cost difference between semi-custom and full custom is meaningful but often smaller than homeowners expect — particularly when you account for the value of perfect fit, superior materials, and the elimination of compromise. During your consultation at Inplace Studio, we provide transparent comparisons so you can make the best decision for your home and budget.

## Orange County Building Requirements

Kitchen remodeling in Orange County requires compliance with local building codes and permit requirements, which vary by city and the scope of the project. Homeowners should be aware of several key considerations.

**Building permits** are required for most kitchen remodels that involve structural changes, electrical modifications, plumbing relocation, or changes to gas lines. Cities like Newport Beach, Laguna Beach, and Irvine each have their own building departments and plan review processes. Permit timelines can range from two weeks to several months depending on the municipality and the complexity of the project.

**Coastal development permits** may be required for homes located within the California Coastal Zone, which affects many properties in Laguna Beach, Dana Point, Newport Beach, and San Clemente. These permits address potential impacts on coastal resources and can add time to the approval process.

**HOA architectural review** is a factor in many Orange County communities, particularly gated and master-planned neighborhoods such as Coto de Caza, Shady Canyon, Pelican Hill, and numerous Irvine communities. Even for interior remodels, some HOAs require notification or approval for construction activity, dumpster placement, and contractor access.

**Title 24 energy compliance** is a California state requirement that affects kitchen remodels involving lighting, HVAC modifications, or window changes. Your contractor and design team must ensure that the finished project meets current energy efficiency standards.

Inplace Studio's familiarity with Orange County's permitting landscape helps our clients navigate these requirements efficiently. While we do not pull permits directly, we work closely with general contractors and expeditors who manage the permitting process, and we ensure that our designs meet all applicable code requirements from the outset.
